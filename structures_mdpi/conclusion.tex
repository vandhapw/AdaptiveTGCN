\indent
This study explored the use of a stochastic TCN enhanced with Adaptive Uncertainty-Aware Attention, Dynamic Weight Adapter, and Feature Importance Learner for multivariate time-series forecasting and anomaly detection in indoor air quality sensor data. The enhanced model was trained on 41,602 sequences and validated on 8,914 sequences, each with 60 timesteps and 40 engineered features. Training converged with early stopping at epoch 55, achieving a best validation loss of 0.000274 (16.97\% improvement from initial loss), demonstrating strong learning capability. Experimental results confirmed that TCN-Stochastic achieved exceptional predictive accuracy (MSE = 0.000212, R$^2$ = 0.9972 for the base model; MSE = 0.000261, R$^2$ = 0.9965 for the enhanced model), effectively quantifying uncertainty through Monte Carlo Dropout during inference.

\indent
The anomaly detection system successfully identified 475 anomalies (5.33\% anomaly rate) in the validation set using an adaptive threshold on hybrid anomaly scores. This detection rate indicates the model's ability to identify genuine outliers without excessive false positives. The detected anomalies correspond to timesteps where indoor air quality patterns deviated significantly from normal behavior, potentially indicating ventilation changes, equipment malfunctions, or unusual environmental events. Feature attribution analysis revealed that VOC (21.8\%), CO\textsubscript{2} (21.7\%), and Humidity (21.7\%) were the primary contributors to detected anomalies, with relatively balanced contributions across sensors and Temperature showing the least influence (15.0\%). Notably, the Dynamic Weight Adapter produced learned weights clustering near 0.5 (mean $\alpha$ = 0.454, std = 0.006), suggesting that the uncertainty signal requires further calibration to drive more meaningful adaptive weighting.

\indent
Future work will extend anomaly detection toward sensor fault detection, focusing on accurately identifying, isolating, and diagnosing faults. The model will also be developed into a hybrid approach by incorporating self-supervised learning to address scenarios involving massive unlabeled data. Additionally, we plan to deploy the trained model for real-time monitoring and integrate it with automated alert systems for proactive indoor air quality management.
